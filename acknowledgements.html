Glossary

Batch effects

Technical sources of potentially confounding variation that are accidentally added to the samples during handling.

Copy number

The number of copies of mitochondrial DNA that are present in a cell.

Gillespie algorithm

A stochastic simulation algorithm that uses probability density functions to determine which reaction will occur next and the time interval until the next reaction.

Heteroplasmy

A per-cell measurement that describes the presence of more than one type of mitochondrial DNA.

Kernel desnsity estimation

A non-parametric method of estimating the probability density function of a random variable.

Linear feedback control model

A mathematical model devised for predicting how populations of mitochondrial DNA change over time.

MERRF (myoclonic epilepsy with ragged red fibers)

A rare mitochondrial disease with varying degrees of severity due to different levels of heteroplasmy.

Mitochondrion

A membrane-bound organelle responsible for energy production. Found in almost all eukaryotic cells.

Mitochondrial DNA

The circular chromosomal genome found with mitochondria.

Mutant mitochondrial DNA

An abnormal variation in DNA that can lead to biochemical and functional defects.

Organelle

A structure with a specialised feature that is suspended in the eukaryotic cell cytoplasm.

Principle component analysis

A statistical procedure that converts values of possibly correlated variables into a set of linearly uncorrelated variables.

The coefficient of determination (R2)

The proportion of variance in one variable that can be predicted from another variable.

Relaxed replication model

A mathematical model devised for predicting how populations of mitochondrial DNA change over time.

Single-cell RNA-seq

A method for identifying and quantifying all genes that are expressed in a single cell.

TMRM

A cell-permeant dye that is readily sequestered by active mitochondria.

Wild-type mitochondrial DNA

Non-mutant DNA molecule capable of performing all healthy functions.
