Summary

Understanding the ways in which mtDNA is regulated in the healthy organellar or cellular state is essential for identifying how diseases manifest themselves in these systems. Varying levels of heteroplasmy and mtDNA copy number have been associated with numerous human diseases (Stewart & Chinnery, 2015). Therefore, an understanding of how diseases lead to copy number and heteroplasmy variations could be used to inform the development new drugs or therapies for mtDNA-associated diseases.

Single-cell RNA-seq

Here, scRNA-seq data obtained from cells with a mutant or wild type MERRF allele was analysed. scRNA-seq can quantify heteroplasmy and identify differences in gene expression profiles for mtDNA and nuclear DNA for cells with varying levels of heteroplasmy. Batch effects can confound true biological variation in scRNA-seq datasets so should be removed. Various batch effect removal methods were utilised and compared. ComBat (Liu & Markatou, 2016) and GLM (Kiselev et al., 2018) were identified as the most effective methods for removing batch effects from the scRNA-seq data set. The results obtained from this analysis highlight the importance of removing batch effects.

Mathematical Modelling

The LFC and the RR models are mathematically distinct models used for modelling mtDNA population dynamics over time. Being able to distinguish between results obtained from either method is important to identify underlying mechanisms driving changes to mtDNA populations. Here, it has been shown that comparing copy numbers obtained during a short time interval is inadequate for distinguishing either model from the other. Other methods, such as decreasing the time interval or independently estimating parameters, could be attempted.
